Welcome To Kerala Tourism

Ernakulam

As a bustling port city with a blend of colonial and modern influences, Ernakulam captivates visitors with its historic Fort Kochi area, where you can explore the centuries-old Chinese fishing nets, colonial-era buildings, and bustling spice markets. The district also offers opportunities for cultural experiences like Kathakali performances and boat rides through the scenic backwaters.

Thiruvananthapuram

The capital city of Kerala, Thiruvananthapuram, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and historic landmarks such as the iconic Padmanabhaswamy Temple and the majestic Napier Museum. Visitors can explore the lush greenery of the city's surrounding areas, including the serene beaches of Kovalam and the tranquil backwaters of Poovar.

Thrissur

Often referred to as the "Cultural Capital of Kerala," Thrissur is famous for its vibrant festivals, including the spectacular Thrissur Pooram. The district is dotted with ancient temples, colorful festivals, and cultural landmarks like the Vadakkunnathan Temple.

Alappuzha

Dubbed the "Venice of the East," Alappuzha is famous for its extensive network of backwaters, where visitors can enjoy enchanting houseboat cruises amidst serene landscapes. The district also hosts the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race and boasts verdant paddy fields.

Malappuram

Nestled between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, Malappuram is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, historic monuments, and pristine beaches. Visitors can explore ancient mosques, lush forests, and the scenic Nilambur Teak Museum

Kozhikode

Steeped in history and natural beauty, Kozhikode is famous for its historic significance as a major trading port and the place where Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama first set foot in India. The district boasts scenic beaches, historic landmarks, and vibrant markets.

View detail
Idukki

Known for its stunning hill stations, dense forests, and wildlife sanctuaries, Idukki is a paradise for nature enthusiasts. The district is home to the famous Idukki Arch Dam, the hill station of Munnar, and the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary.

View detail
Wayanad

Tucked away in the Western Ghats, Wayanad mesmerizes visitors with its verdant forests, cascading waterfalls, and lush tea estates. Nature enthusiasts can explore the district's diverse wildlife at the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary and trek to the majestic peaks of Chembra and Banasura.

View detail
Kannur

Located in the northern part of Kerala, Kannur is known for its rich cultural heritage, historic landmarks, and pristine beaches. The district is famous for its Theyyam rituals, a traditional art form that combines dance, music, and vibrant costumes.

View detail
Kasargod

Kasaragod: Situated in the northernmost part of Kerala, Kasaragod is renowned for its serene beaches, historic forts, and cultural diversity. The district is known for its unique Theyyam rituals, a traditional art form that attracts visitors from far and wide.

Kollam

Known as the "Cashew Capital of the World," Kollam is famous for its cashew industry and historic significance as a major trading port. Visitors can explore the ancient temples, serene backwaters, and picturesque beaches like Kollam Beach and Thangassery Beach.

Palakkad

Known for its scenic beauty and historic significance, Palakkad is home to the picturesque Silent Valley National Park and the majestic Palakkad Fort. The district also boasts lush paddy fields, serene rivers, and the serene hill station of Nelliyampathy.

View detail
Pathanamthitta

Nestled amidst the Western Ghats, Pathanamthitta is known for its religious significance, with the Sabarimala Ayyappa Temple attracting millions of pilgrims annually. The district is also blessed with lush forests, serene rivers, and the scenic hill station of Konni.

Kottayam

Renowned for its rubber plantations and lush greenery, Kottayam is dotted with serene backwaters and picturesque landscapes. Visitors can explore the district's historic temples, churches, and the scenic Vembanad Lake.
